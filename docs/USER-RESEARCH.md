# ablefy User Research

> Compiled: 2026-05-04
> Purpose: Inform UX redesign of the ablefy signup → course created flow
> Scope: German and English sources — blogs, review platforms, YouTube, community forums

---

## Summary

ablefy (rebranded from elopage in October 2024) is the dominant German-language platform for coaches, trainers, and digital creators selling online courses, memberships, and digital products. It has ~70,000+ sellers and 9M+ buyers in the DACH market.

**Overall sentiment:** Mixed-positive on Capterra/OMR (3.4–4.4/5), mixed-negative on Trustpilot where operational complaints dominate. Bloggers and affiliates tend toward positive because they benefit from referral programs; unsponsored user reviews surface sharper pain points.

**Top 5 findings relevant to the onboarding redesign:**

1. **The dashboard overwhelms new users on first login.** Multiple independent sources use the exact word "überwältigend" (overwhelming) to describe the first post-signup experience. The platform does not guide users to a single, obvious first action.
2. **Product creation for beginners is described as "complex" and unintuitive.** There are no ready-made page templates — users must build them from scratch. A GetResponse reviewer wrote: "Für Anfänger*innen finde ich das System etwas kompliziert und schade ist auch, dass es keine Vorlagen gibt." Martina L. on Capterra: *"Die Kurserstellung wäre mir ohne Masterclass und Tutorials niemals gelungen"* (course creation would have been impossible without external tutorials).
3. **The path from signup to a published, sellable course requires 7+ distinct configuration steps** spread across product details, content creation, payment page setup, upsells, delivery settings, payout account, and product approval — with no clear progress indicator.
4. **Critical settings cannot be changed after the first sale** (access duration, refund conditions), yet first-time users are not prominently warned of this.
5. **Product approval ("Verkaufsfreigabe") takes up to 72 hours** after a seller completes setup — an opaque delay that disrupts time-sensitive launches and is nowhere surfaced during onboarding.

## Onboarding and Signup-Specific Feedback

This section focuses on the signup → course created flow, which is the scope of this case study.

### What the Flow Looks Like (Reconstructed from Research)

1. **Registration** — Email + password only. No credit card required. 14-day free trial. Described as "ganz einfach" (very simple) by GetResponse reviewer.
2. **3-step demo** — Platform provides a quick guided demo: select product type → enter product name + cover → configure basic pricing. Superficially simple.
3. **Account configuration (pre-selling requirements)** — Before a product can go live, users must configure: shop name + URL, payout bank account, payment providers (Stripe verification takes several days), tax/legal settings, email configuration. These are not prominently surfaced in the initial demo.
4. **Product creation (7-step workflow)** — General settings → product details → course content (page builder) → payment page → upsells → delivery settings → additional settings.
5. **Product approval ("Verkaufsfreigabe")** — After submission, up to 72 hours review by ablefy before the product can be sold. Creators are not warned about this during setup.
6. **Dashboard post-login** — Described as "ziemlich überwältigend" (pretty overwhelming) by first-time users due to the number of menu points and functions.

### Specific Onboarding Friction Points (Evidence-Based)

**F1 — False simplicity of the 3-step demo**
The initial 3-step demo creates an expectation that setup is fast. Users then discover the real prerequisite checklist (bank account, Stripe, tax settings, USt. ID) is separate and ungated. Multiple users report surprise at the gap between the demo's simplicity and the actual requirements.

**F2 — No page templates for course content**
"Es gibt keine Vorlagen, die man einfach benutzen kann. Die Templates musst Du erst selbst zusammenbasteln." (GetResponse review) — New creators must build every page block from scratch, which is daunting for a first product. Competitors like Coachy and Teachable provide ready-made templates.

**F3 — Course creation is impossible without external tutorials**
Martina L. on Capterra (4/5): *"Die Kurserstellung wäre mir ohne Masterclass und Tutorials niemals gelungen."* The existence of a paid "Masterclass" to complete basic product creation is a significant signal of onboarding failure. The platform directs new users to the "ablefy Academy" — but not all Academy content is free.

**F4 — Irreversible settings not flagged upfront**
Certain fields (access duration, refund conditions, certain pricing structures) cannot be changed after the first sale. Losprobiert.de explicitly warns about this in their tutorial. If a new user doesn't know this in advance, they may configure incorrectly and be locked in.

**F5 — Payment provider setup is a blocker with no timeline visibility**
Stripe identity verification and payout account setup can take "mehrere Tage" (several days). Users starting a launch do not know this is required until they attempt to publish. The guide "channi.at" explicitly says: "Fange frühzeitig mit der Einrichtung an" — begin early — implying this is a learned lesson, not an onboarding-stage warning.

**F6 — Email mismatch creates invisible buyers**
After purchase, buyers whose purchase email differs from their account email do not see the product in their account. This is the #1 reported support ticket topic for ablefy sellers (losprobiert.de). The root cause is in the signup/checkout flow's lack of account linkage transparency.

**F7 — 72-hour product approval delay is invisible during setup**
*"Verkaufsfreigabe...kann bis zu 72 Stunden dauern"* — This is a hard blocker for creators with planned launch dates. No onboarding step warns about it. Users discover it only after completing all setup steps.

**F8 — First dashboard login overwhelm**
*"Wenn du dich das allererste Mal bei Ablefy einloggst, kann das erstmal ziemlich überwältigend wirken."* (Everyblue) — The dashboard presents the full feature surface immediately with no progressive disclosure, no first-run wizard beyond the 3-step demo, and no contextual "next step" indicator tied to the user's actual progress state.

**F9 — Mobile responsiveness is manual**
In the page builder, desktop and mobile layouts are separate. Users must duplicate elements for each view. This adds hidden complexity to what appears to be a simple drag-and-drop editor. (lenakallemeier.de)

**F10 — Design customization gated behind expensive tiers**
Essential plan (€39/mo) offers "keinerlei Designanpassungen" (no design customization). Creators who want even basic brand colors or custom fonts must upgrade to Pro (€129/mo). Users see a mismatch between the attractive-looking demo and what they can actually build on the plan they sign up for. (virtual-support.de)

### What Users Praise About the Onboarding

- Registration itself (email + password, no credit card) is frictionless and fast
- The 14-day free trial with no auto-upgrade is appreciated: "Du wirst nicht automatisch auf ein kostenpflichtiges Paket hochgestuft"
- ablefy Academy provides video walkthroughs (though not all free)
- Support is accessible via phone, WhatsApp, and email — praised by several users for resolving setup questions
- Dashboard has a "nächste Schritte" (next steps) section that provides some guidance post-login
- The 3-step demo creates a sense of early momentum before the real complexity surfaces

### Onboarding Summary for Redesign

The core tension: ablefy's onboarding **promises simplicity** ("put products online in minutes") but **delivers complexity** (7-step workflow, multiple account prerequisites, opaque approval delay, no templates). First-time users — particularly those like the "Alex" persona (AI influencer, 10–15 min evaluation window) — hit the complexity wall immediately after the demo and either seek external tutorials or abandon.

The **10–15 minute window** of a creator like Alex is insufficient to complete the actual setup. The platform's design does not acknowledge or work with this constraint. A redesigned flow must surface the critical path (what must be done first, what can wait), provide templates that produce a working product quickly, and be transparent about prerequisites (Stripe verification, approval delay) before users discover them as blockers.
